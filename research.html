My Research

Trans-oral robotic surgery (TORS) specialized 3mm surgical tools and surgical system - SickKids Hospital - Center for Image Guided Innovation and Therapeutic Intervntion (CIGITI)

Skills Used: Solidworks, Python, C++, C#, ROS, Unity, Robotic Simulation

Surgical procedures in or through the oral cavity, particularly in pediatrics, are challenging due to the size and shape of the cavity. This necessitates the development of novel robotic surgical tools and systems specialized for trans-oral surgeries, such as in cleft palate repair. I led the research and development of a novel 4 arm surgical system and elbowed 3mm diameter surgical tools for trans-oral robotic surgery. I designed and assisted in building numerous iterations of the tool (patent pending), as well as the development of a bimanual stereoscopic robotic simulator for tool validation and verification.

Novel bottlebrush elastomers (BBEs) for non-invasive neural interfacing - University of Toronto - Microfluidics and BioMEMs Lab (MBL)

Skills Used: Microfluidics, soft material fabrication, mechanical/electrochemical material characterization, Python

Existing rigid neural interfacing device pose a high risk of biological rejection and lack biocompatibility, which can lead to adverse health effects in the long term. The major discrepancy in softness and stretchability in electrode and biological material can is addressed using novel PEDOT:PSS and PDMS based BBEs exhibiting high conductivity and softness. I was responsible for the characterization, testing, and optimization of BBE recipes, achieving a final Young's modulus of 1kPa (>4000x softer vs. conventional electrodes) and >250 S/m conductivity. I also collected and processed EMG and ECG data from electrode testing for comparison to existing electrodes, exhibiting better or equal performance across tests.
